##  [Misconfigured domain issues](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/managing-dns-records#misconfigured-domain-issues)[](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/managing-dns-records#misconfigured-domain-issues)
When you add a domain to Vercel that you have purchased from a third-party DNS provider, you may see an Invalid Configuration alert. There are many reasons why this could be the case:
  * You need to configure the [DNS](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/managing-dns-records#common-dns-issues) records of your domain with your DNS provider so they can be used with your project. To resolve this, follow the steps to [configure your domain](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/add-a-domain#configure-the-domain).
  * If your domain is in use by another Vercel account, you may be prompted to [verify access to the domain](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/add-a-domain#verify-domain-access) by adding a TXT record. This will not move the domain into your account, but will allow you to use it in your project.
  * There was an issue generating the SSL certificate for your domain. The most common reason for this is [missing CAA records](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/managing-dns-records#missing-caa-records). For information on other issues that may cause this, see the [common SSL certificate issues](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/managing-dns-records#common-ssl-certificate-issues) section.
  * You have configured [wildcard subdomains](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/add-a-domain#using-wildcard-domain) on your project, but their nameservers aren’t with Vercel. When using a wildcard domain, you must use the [nameservers method](https://vercel.com/docs/domains/troubleshooting#configuring-nameservers-for-wildcard-domains).
